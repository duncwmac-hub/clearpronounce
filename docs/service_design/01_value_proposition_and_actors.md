# 01 – Value proposition and actors

## 1. Service in one sentence

**ClearPronounce** is a pronunciation-aware vocabulary service that turns any English text (e.g. from a textbook or meeting) into a ranked word list with IPA and L1-friendly respellings, so Polish-speaking learners and their teachers can see which sounds are new, prioritise practice, and speak more clearly without changing curriculum.

---

## 2. Value proposition

### For the learner (Polish speaker, e.g. professional or student)

| Dimension | Proposition |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | “I see which words in this text use sounds I don’t yet have, I get a readable respelling to approximate them, and I can tick sounds I already know and see how much of the text I can ‘own’.” |
| **Emotional** | “I’m not guessing; I know what to work on. The tool feels affirming, not judgmental.” |
| **Outcome** | Fewer high-impact substitutions, more intelligibility and confidence when speaking English. |

### For the teacher (e.g. language school, in-company trainer)

| Dimension | Proposition |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | “I paste text from the coursebook or a meeting and get a pronunciation-aware vocab list plus export. I can use it for Unit Zero, homework, or differentiation.” |
| **Emotional** | “I’m supported, not overloaded. The tool fits my workflow.” |
| **Outcome** | Less ad-hoc pronunciation prep; clearer focus on sounds that matter for this cohort and text. |

### For the provider (ClearPronounce)

| Dimension | Proposition |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | A focused product that integrates with existing materials and scales to more L1s and modalities (e.g. AI tutor, phoneme unlocker). |
| **Outcome** | Sustainable revenue (B2C support, B2B pilots, later paid tiers) and a reputation for evidence-based, learner-affirming design. |

---

## 3. Primary and secondary actors

| Actor | Role | Primary / secondary | Key job to be done |
|-------|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Polish-speaking learner** | Uses pronunciation guidance to speak more clearly. | **Primary** | “Before I use this text (class, meeting), I want to know which words need new sounds and how they sound, so I can prepare and feel confident.” |
| **Teacher / trainer** | Prepares materials and uses ClearPronounce output in class or for homework. | **Primary** | “I want to turn my chosen text into a pronunciation-aware vocab list and export it, so I can run Unit Zero or targeted practice without reinventing the wheel.” |
| **School / institution** | Decides on pilots, materials, and budget. | **Secondary** | “I want a low-friction, credible way to improve pronunciation outcomes for our learners.” |
| **ClearPronounce (provider)** | Builds, operates, and evolves the service. | **Internal** | Deliver value reliably, learn from use, and generate revenue. |

**Design implication:** The service must satisfy both learner and teacher; the current MVP is teacher-facing (paste text → list + export) with learner-facing value (respelling, coverage game). Future “teacher temperament” and “how the teacher teaches” are about tailoring the **same** service to different teaching styles and learner preferences.

---

## 4. Success criteria (early phase)

- **Usage:** Teachers or learners paste text and use the list (and/or CSV) in a real context (e.g. one class, one pilot).
- **Affirmation:** The experience is described as helpful and non-shaming (e.g. “coverage” and “sounds you already know” feel positive).
- **Revenue:** At least one payment (donation, pilot, or deliverable) as proof of willingness to pay.
- **Learning:** Evidence of where the journey breaks (e.g. onboarding, export, mobile) to inform the next service design audit (post–first use).

---

## 5. Out of scope (for this audit)

- Detailed UX of a future “Michel Thomas–style” tutor or phoneme-unlocker flow (those are later journey extensions).
- Full B2B sales and onboarding (mapped when that channel is in scope).
- Comparison with specific competitors (audit is service-centric).
