# ClearPronounce – Supplementary pronunciation guides for textbooks and workbooks

## What ClearPronounce does

ClearPronounce adds pronunciation support tailored to a student’s first language alongside the materials they are already using in class.

It does not replace coursebooks or require a new curriculum. It provides supplementary pronunciation guides that accompany existing textbooks and workbooks, offering clear respellings that guide learners on how English should actually sound.

## The problem it addresses

- English spelling does not reliably represent English sound (consider *though / through / thought*).
- Learners naturally apply the reading rules of their own language when encountering English words. This leads to predictable pronunciation patterns which, if unaddressed, become fixed habits.
- Most mainstream textbooks are written to serve learners from all first-language backgrounds equally. While this makes them scalable, it also means that specific pronunciation challenges linked to particular L1 sound systems are rarely addressed directly.

## How ClearPronounce helps

ClearPronounce intervenes at the point where spelling meets sound.

For key vocabulary and high-frequency phrases students are studying, it provides pronunciation guidance designed for their native language background.

These guides are presented and learned as a structured **“Unit Zero”** before starting the relevant chapter or unit, helping to ensure learners know how to pronounce key words clearly before they encounter their standard spelling in the textbook.

The aim is not accent removal. The aim is to reduce high-impact substitutions that affect clarity and to reduce learner uncertainty about how written English maps to speech.

When learners are no longer guessing how a word should sound, they speak with greater confidence and require less corrective intervention. This reduces cognitive load in speaking tasks and supports faster fluency development.

## Practical advantages for schools

- Integrates with existing materials  
- Does not require timetable changes  
- Suitable for mixed-ability classes  
- Best suited to cohorts with a limited number of L1 backgrounds during the pilot phase  
- Offers measurable gains in intelligibility and speaking confidence

## Current scope

- Spanish has been the prototype case, with developed respelling models and sound-mapping structures.
- Polish models are in active development.
- Additional language backgrounds can be developed by identifying predictable reading-to-sound transfer patterns.
